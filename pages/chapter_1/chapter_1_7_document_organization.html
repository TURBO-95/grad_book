1.7 Document Organization

This graduation project book is structured into six comprehensive chapters, supplemented by extensive appendices, to reflect the scientific and engineering methodologies employed during the development of IntgraServeAI. A brief outline of the document's organization is provided below:

  • Chapter 1: Introduction. Introduces the project's background, defines the problem statement, outlines the SMART objectives, and delineates the project's scope and contributions.
  • Chapter 2: Literature Review. Provides a detailed analysis of the prevailing theories, historical context, and recent advancements in Large Language Models, RAG systems, and enterprise automation, establishing the theoretical foundation for the project.
  • Chapter 3: System Analysis. Explores the comprehensive analysis of the proposed system, detailing the functional and non-functional requirements, user scenarios, and the overarching system architecture.
  • Chapter 4: System Design. Delves into the technical blueprints of IntgraServeAI, presenting class diagrams, database schemas, API designs, and the internal algorithms governing the Action Engine and RAG pipelines.
  • Chapter 5: System Implementation and Testing. Discusses the mapping of design to actual code, highlighting sample application configurations, the testing methodologies applied to ensure reliability, and an evaluation of the final results against the initial objectives.
  • Chapter 6: Conclusion and Future Work. Summarizes the entire research and development effort, reflects on the limitations encountered, and proposes actionable avenues for future research and system expansion.
  • Appendices: Contains supplementary materials, including detailed Use Case Specifications (Appendix A), Sequence Diagrams (Appendix B), and the complete API Reference Documentation (Appendix C).
